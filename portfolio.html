Taxing Green Beans: Economic Analysis of Green Coffee Bean Imports

December 2025 Co-author: Rowdy Roberson
Team Research

Conducted an empirical economic analysis examining the impact of U.S. tariff increases on green coffee bean imports. Applied a Gravity model framework using Poisson Pseudo Maximum Likelihood (PPML) estimation to analyze how tariff policy changes affected import flows from major coffee-exporting countries. The study identified short-run stockpiling behavior and anticipation effects as firms responded to announced tariff increases between April and August 2025.

Methodology

  • Gravity model with PPML estimation
  • Fixed effects for multilateral resistance
  • Analysis of 2,289 trade observations (expanded to 5,656 with zeros)
  • Examination of Executive Order tariff implementations
  • Time-lagged announcement effects (1-2 month lags)

Key Findings

  • Estimated elasticity of 2.66 for tariff-inclusive price term
  • Short-run stockpiling behavior before tariff execution
  • Anticipation effects from policy uncertainty
  • Shipping lag considerations (4-8 weeks for ocean freight)
  • Import spike in May following April announcements

Forecasting the Housing Price Index for Washington State

December 2025 Solo Project
Independent Research

Developed comprehensive forecasting models for Washington State's All-Transactions House Price Index using both univariate (ARIMA) and multivariate (VAR) time-series approaches. The project utilized annual data from 1984-2024 provided by the Federal Reserve Bank of St. Louis (FRED) and examined relationships between housing prices, unemployment, population growth, and real median household income.

Methodology

  • Augmented Dickey-Fuller (ADF) stationarity testing
  • ARIMA(2,1,0) univariate model
  • Vector Autoregression (VAR) multivariate model
  • Impulse Response Functions (IRF) analysis
  • Shock experiments with ±2 standard deviations

Key Findings

  • VAR model produced more conservative, plausible forecasts
  • Unemployment showed strongest correlation (-0.363) with housing price growth
  • Population and income growth showed minimal direct correlation
  • Shocks dissipate after approximately 2 periods
  • 95% confidence intervals narrowed significantly in multivariate model
